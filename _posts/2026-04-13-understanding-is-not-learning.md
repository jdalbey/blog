TIL: Understanding is not learning.  
Cognitive Scientist Maryellen MacDonald explains that the sensation we experience of understanding something is not actually learning. Example: you watch a documentary about bees and see a colony being "split" and understand how a new colony can be created from an existing colony in about six weeks. It makes sense to you. You "understand" it. But the next time a conversation about bee hives comes up will you recall this particular fact? Maybe not, unless you have taken deliberate steps to turn understanding into learning, such as writing it down, telling someone else about it, or deliberately repeating the fact to yourself with your inner dialog. Learning happens without an associated sensation, often while we sleep, when the brain sorts out trivial stuff from "worth remembering" stuff.
